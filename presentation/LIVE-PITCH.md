# Penelope Ontology — live pitch plan

## Positioning

**For writers, narrative designers, quest teams, and professional GMs who need a story to keep responding after the prompt ends, Penelope is a portable causal story simulator. It resolves what a choice changes before asking a model to write the next scene.**

The sale is not “better AI prose.” The sale is **a world that remembers the creator's choice**.

## Three-minute stage structure

### 0:00–0:25 · Hook

“People say Codex builds but does not write. I started with a different question: what if the weakness is not the sentence, but the world behind it? A fluent paragraph is still broken if the wrong character knows the secret or yesterday's choice has no cost.”

### 0:25–0:55 · Define the product

“Penelope Ontology is a causal story simulator for creators. You load a bounded world, make a choice, and inspect how knowledge, motives, relationships, risks, and possible endings move before prose is written.”

### 0:55–1:35 · Deliver the memorable proof

“Here, Odysseus has returned to Ithaca in disguise. I send the hostile servant Melantho away to protect his identity. The request succeeds: privacy rises. But Melantho remembers being excluded and starts investigating offstage. I removed a witness. I created an investigator.”

Pause after the last line. Show the causal receipt, not a feature list.

### 1:35–2:00 · Show creator control

“I can return to the same checkpoint and contain Eurycleia's reaction instead. The same past reaches a different ending. Penelope compares changed world state, not paragraph similarity. A rejected or ambiguous creator proposal changes nothing.”

### 2:00–2:25 · Prove portability

“Ithaca is only one World Pack. I can switch to Oz, import a creator-owned pack, or use World Forge to turn a short premise into an approved five-scene episode. The cast, secrets, actions, and endings change. The engine does not.”

### 2:25–2:50 · Explain the AI role honestly

“Codex helped turn my tacit writing judgment into schemas, validators, interfaces, and replay. The model can propose expression, but deterministic state owns facts and consequences, and the creator owns canon and taste.”

### 2:50–3:00 · Close

“Penelope does not just generate the next paragraph. It gives every choice a world that remembers. Fork the world, not just the paragraph.”

## Thirty-second fallback

“Fluent AI prose still breaks when the wrong character knows a secret or a choice has no cost. Penelope Ontology resolves the world before it writes the scene. In our Odyssey demo, removing a hostile witness protects Odysseus for one moment—but creates an offstage investigator. Return to the same checkpoint and the world can reach another ending. The same engine also runs Oz and creator-owned World Packs. Penelope gives writers, narrative teams, and GMs a world that remembers their choices.”

## Live demo choreography

1. Open with the Book 19 pack already loaded.
2. Use the prepared C route; do not type the full interview on stage.
3. Reveal aftermath receipts one at a time.
4. Pause on `You removed the witness. You created an investigator.`
5. Show Fork Compare for ten seconds.
6. Switch once to Oz, then show the World Forge entry point.
7. End on the product thesis, not the test count.

## Likely judge questions

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| Is this just RAG or a world bible? | No. A pack supplies bounded knowledge, but deterministic transitions also resolve costs, clocks, relationships, endings, and checkpoint lineage. |
| Is it just branching fiction? | The current episode is bounded, but branches are compared through typed state and declared reactions rather than only prewritten paragraph alternatives. |
| Can I use my own setting? | Yes, through a strict World Pack or guided World Forge. Sensitive unpublished work should use local self-hosting. |
| Does the AI control canon? | No. The model proposes expression. State transitions and creator approval control what becomes authoritative. |
| Is this a full autonomous simulation? | No. It is a bounded rehearsal engine. That limit keeps consequences inspectable and the demo honest. |
