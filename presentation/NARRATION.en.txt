Codex cannot write? That is the shorthand: Codex builds; it does not write. But what if the real failure begins before the sentence?

AI can produce fluent prose and still forget who knows the secret, erase the cost of a choice, or move every character with the narrator's knowledge.

Penelope Ontology gives Codex a world it must obey. Load a bounded World Pack. Each character keeps separate knowledge, motives, relationships, and risks. The creator chooses. Deterministic rules resolve what changes. The model writes only after the world has answered.

In The Odyssey, I send Melantho away to protect Odysseus's identity. Privacy rises. But Melantho remembers the exclusion and investigates offstage. I removed a witness. I created an investigator.

Return to the same checkpoint, contain Eurycleia's reaction, and the same past reaches a different ending. Penelope compares changed state, not paragraph similarity.

Ithaca is only one pack. Oz and creator-owned worlds use the same engine. World Forge turns a short premise into an approved five-scene episode.

Codex helped turn a writer's tacit judgment into contracts, validators, interfaces, and replay. Penelope does not take authorship. It gives every choice a world that remembers.
