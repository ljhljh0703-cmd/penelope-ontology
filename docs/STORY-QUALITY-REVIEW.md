# Story quality review

Penelope's machine gates prove bounded structure, not literary quality. A scene can pass schema, scope, and causality checks and still feel flat. Public writing-quality claims therefore require a creator review of the actual generated prose.

## Review unit

Review one complete three-scene run, not isolated sentences:

1. **The Signal** — pressure exists before the human chooses.
2. **The Cost / The Alarm** — the first choice returns as both benefit and cost.
3. **The Payoff / The Public Price** — the central question closes and the chosen policy owns its consequence.

The fixture and a live Codex run are different evidence. A fixture score cannot be reported as live-model quality, and a successful process trace cannot substitute for prose review.

## Creator rubric

Score each item from 1 (failed) to 5 (strong), then record one concrete sentence of evidence.

| Criterion | Review question |
|---|---|
| Voice | Can Penelope, Telemachus, and Eurycleia be distinguished without dialogue tags doing all the work? |
| Viewpoint | Does each scene stay inside its declared limited-third focal character? |
| Subtext | Does dialogue carry disagreement or relationship pressure instead of explaining the contract? |
| Rhythm | Do observation, pressure, and decision use meaningfully different sentence rhythms? |
| Image control | Does the covered lamp recur with changed meaning rather than invite unrelated decorative motifs? |
| Spatial continuity | Can the reader follow every movement and distinguish new evidence from props the characters brought? |
| Epistemic restraint | Is uncertainty dramatized through action, refusal, or dialogue instead of narrator-style knowledge reports? |
| Causal satisfaction | Can the reader point to the earlier choice that made the later discovery or loss possible? |
| Fair cost | Does each branch receive a real advantage and a proportional price without authorial punishment? |
| Progress | Does every scene irreversibly change what the characters can do or know? |
| Choice ownership | Does the scene stop before the next offered action, leaving its named actor and outcome genuinely undecided? |
| Closure | Does Scene 3 answer the red-sail question while leaving at most one honest residual hook? |
| Playability | Can a GM or narrative designer continue immediately from the final pressure without rewriting the scene? |

## Verdict

- `accept`: no criterion below 3, and the creator judges the complete arc usable without prose repair.
- `revise`: the causal arc works, but one or more prose passages need regeneration or a style-profile adjustment.
- `reject`: the run breaks viewpoint, knowledge scope, choice responsibility, or small-arc closure.

Record the transport, requested model, actual model when independently reported, output hash, whether prose was manually edited, criterion scores, evidence sentences, and verdict. Do not publish raw private prompts, credentials, or unrelated Codex task context.
